Early Stage Cardiovascular Clinical Diagnosis: Approach of Convolutional Neural Network to Classify the Level of Left Ventricular Ejection Fraction from Echcardiography Electronic Health Records

Research Abstract

This research study applied a deep learning model to classify the levels of left ventricular ejection fraction using clinical stages data – text data records in near 950 echocardiography scan reports from different cardiovascular patients in a Cardiovascular Wards. Currently, Cardiologists and Cardiothoracic surgeons are manually reviewing the clinical reports especially patients echocardiography reports to diagnose the cardiovascular clinical stage of left ventricular ejection fraction (LVEF). Although, there are several ways have been made to automate this cardiovascular clinical processes to extract information from clinical reports, especially from echocardiography reports, but currently there are limited research studies for analysing the cardiovascular clinical trials. This research study focused on to establish some baseline performance measures using a 1- dimensional convolutional neural network (CNN) and determine strategies for improving classification precision. Currently, we are in first stage of development of the application. The deep learning model results were promising but were unable to exceed precision or F-scores of 80% for mild, moderate, or severe LVEF; irrespective of how the training data was sampled, how deeply connected the neural network layers were, or well the model was tuned. This solution is strongly ropes the need to develop a break apart algorithm that could help captures conceptual, contextual, and measurement features from ECHO reports.

Research Dominas: Cardiovascular Diseases, Clinical Trails, Electronic Health Records (EHR), Echocardiography, Natural Language Processing (NLP), Text Classification

Research Justification

An intelligent, reliable, and systematic method for extracting ejection fraction values from ECHO reports would benefit patients, clinicians, and the wider health system. For patients with heart failure, it is vital for clinicians to obtain initial outpatient cardiology evaluations in a timely manner. Manually processing ECHO reports is time consuming and costly to the health system, while rule-based data extraction methods can lack in generalisability, accuracy, and reliability. Therefore, this study takes the opportunity to utilise advanced methods for accurately stratifying levels of LVEF from ECHO reports; to improve the efficiency of the clinician decision-making process based on cardiovascular risk. This study will explore the use of natural language processing (NLP) for text classification of LVEF using free-text data from ECHO reports.
